The Journey of a Single Vote — The Moment Secrecy Is Born
Five Steps That Build Trust
①
Identity Verification
Enter your phone number and the OTP received by text message.
The identity verification department confirms your eligibility in the voter registry.
②
Ticket Issued — And the Connection Is Severed
Once eligibility is confirmed, an 'original ticket' is issued.
From this moment, the connection between the verification department and the voter's screen is permanently severed.
After this point, the verification department has no way of knowing what the voter selects.
✂ This is where secret voting begins
③
Vote Submitted
Select your choice at the polling station and confirm through a verification popup before final submission.
The polling station knows 'this ticket voted for option A,' but never knew who held the ticket.
④
Ticket Exchange — The Core of Secrecy
The moment the vote is submitted, the polling station marks the original ticket as 'used' and instantly creates a brand new ticket.
This new ticket is never recorded in any log at the polling station — it is placed into the vote certificate and delivered, then forgotten.
Since no record of 'original ticket ↔ new ticket' exists, even the polling station cannot later trace 'what certificate this person received.'
★ At this very moment — no link connecting identity and vote content remains anywhere in the world
⑤
Vote Certificate Issued
A vote certificate containing the new ticket is delivered simultaneously to the voter's phone and the counting station.
The voter copies and saves the public confirmation number from this certificate.
After the election, this number can be used to directly verify your vote on the verification page.
